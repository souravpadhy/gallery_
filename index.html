OSHO
Rajneesh, also known as Acharya Rajneesh,
Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh, and
later as Osho,
was an Indian godman, philosopher,
mystic,
and founder of the Rajneesh
movement.
He was viewed as a controversial new
religious movement
leader during his life
INDIAN
PHILOSOPHERS
J
KRISHNAMURTI
Jiddu Krishnamurti was an Indian
philosopher, speaker, writer, and
spiritual figure. Adopted by members of
the Theosophical tradition as a child,
he was raised to fill the advanced role of
World Teacher.
ACHARYA
PRASHANT
Acharya Prashant is an Indian philosopher
, author and Advaita teacher. He teaches
seventeen forms of Gita
and sixty forms of Upanishads.
SWAMI
VIVEKANANDA
Swami Vivekananda, born Narendranath
Datta, was an Indian Hindu monk,
philosopher, author, religious teacher,
and the chief disciple of the Indian
mystic Ramakrishna.
RAMAN
MAHARSHI
Ramana Maharshi was an Indian Hindu
sage and jivanmukta. He was born
Venkataraman Iyer, but is mostly known
by the name Bhagavan Sri Ramana Maharshi.
GAUTAM
BUDHHA
Siddhartha Gautama, most commonly
referred to as the Buddha,was a wandering
ascetic and religious teacher who lived
in South Asia during the 6th or 5th century.
MAHAVIR
JAIN
Mahavira, also known as Vardhamana,
was the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism.
He was the spiritual successor of the
23rd Tirthankara Parshvanatha.
ADI
SHANKARA
Adi Shankara, also called Adi Shankara,
was an Indian Vedic scholar
and teacher
of Advaita Vedanta. Reliable information
on Shankara's actual life is scanty,
and his true impact lies in
his "iconic representation of Hindu religion and culture,"
ACHARYA
CHANAKYA
Chanakya was an ancient Indian
polymath who was active as a teacher,
author, strategist, philosopher, economist,
jurist, and politician.
SRI
AUROBINDO
Sri Aurobindo was an Indian philosopher,
yogi, maharishi, poet, and Indian
nationalist.
He was also a journalist,
editing newspapers
such as Bande Mataram.
